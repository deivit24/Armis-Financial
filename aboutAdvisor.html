David Salazar, MSc


After playing tennis for most of my adolescent life, I was fortunate enough to receive a full-scholarship to attend Lipscomb University in Nashville, TN. Shortly after receiving my B.A. in Accounting with 150 credit hours, I followed my passion which was tennis. I had an amazing opportunity to be one of the assistant men’s tennis coach at the United States Military Academy at West Point. I probably learned more from the cadets that are serving our country than they did from me during my tenure there. I then went to the University of Durham in the United Kingdom to pursue a Master’s in Financial Management while coaching and playing for their tennis team. It was at the point when I knew that I wanted to become a financial advisor.

When I came back to the states I immediately started working for a huge financial service firm. That is when I fell in love with my job but I slowly became to realize that I was limited in providing true value that wasn’t conflicted with my firm’s interest. The more research I did, the more I found out that virtually every firm operates in the similar manner. I had a choice to make. Either quit my dream job and start a different profession or start my own Independent firm and actually make a difference. I chose the latter.
